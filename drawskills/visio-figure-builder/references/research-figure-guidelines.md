# Research Figure Guidelines (for Visio drawskills)

## Reliable defaults for paper-friendly diagrams

- Prefer clean, high-contrast, low-decoration diagrams.
- Keep typography consistent across all panels.
- Avoid red/green-only distinctions; use colorblind-safe contrasts.
- Keep label text horizontal and readable at final print size.

## Suggested defaults for this project

- Font: Times New Roman
- Body label size: 11 pt
- Secondary annotation: 10 pt
- Border width: 1.0–1.4 pt
- Connector width: 0.9–1.2 pt
- Fill colors: very light tones (e.g., 230–250 RGB range)

## Journal-facing notes (generalized)

- IEEE/Elsevier workflows often emphasize high-resolution/vector outputs and readability after scaling.
- Nature-style guidance emphasizes consistency, accessibility, and avoiding clutter.
- For submission, always check target venue’s exact artwork policy before final export.

## Final quality checklist

1. Text readable at expected final figure size
2. Uniform font family and sizing hierarchy
3. Consistent arrow directions and connector style
4. Alignment and spacing visually balanced
5. Colors distinguishable in grayscale/colorblind views
6. No decorative elements without semantic meaning
